About Jarumi Ventures Africa

Building resilient, revenue-generating, and impact-driven institutions across Africa

Our Identity

Jarumi Ventures Africa is a strategic advisory and systems-strengthening firm supporting nonprofits and social impact organizations to transition from donor dependency to sustainability. We bring private-sector precision to purpose-driven work, empowering organizations to strengthen their systems, diversify their revenue, and align their strategy for scale.

With a focus on sustainable practices, we equip nonprofits with the strategic tools, systems, and donor-readiness support needed to scale their impact, sustain operations, and inspire long-term donor confidence.

Our Mission

To empower purpose-driven organizations with systems and strategies that scale impact sustainably, transforming fragile organizations into resilient, future-proof institutions.

Our Vision

A thriving African social sector built on resilience, accountability, and sustainability, where every nonprofit has the capacity to generate revenue, create jobs, and scale their missions independently.

Our Story & The African Context

Jarumi Ventures Africa was founded to bridge a critical gap in Africa's development ecosystem. Across the continent, thousands of nonprofits are doing critical work but lack the business acumen and operational stability to scale or survive donor fatigue.

With over 150,000 registered NGOs across Africa (World Association of NGOs), and millions more community-based organizations, the need for nonprofit sustainability support is urgent. These organizations face increasing pressure to operate sustainably as donor fatigue grows and funders demand stronger accountability.

While impactful in mission, most organizations particularly small to mid sized NGOs and CBOs struggle with weak revenue models, unstructured operations, and a lack of strategic direction. Jarumi's intervention shifts these organizations from fragile to future-proof, unlocking their potential to generate revenue, create jobs, and scale their missions sustainably.

The Market Gap We Address

Across Africa, the social sector faces a critical sustainability challenge. While the competitive landscape includes accelerators, consultants, and networks offering capacity building, most provide short-term, generic training programs with little follow-up. There is a significant gap in embedded, long-term business development support that's adapted to nonprofit realities.

Donor Dependency

Most nonprofits rely heavily on external funding, making them vulnerable to shifts in donor priorities or funding cycles.

Weak Infrastructure

Organizations often operate without clear strategic plans, strong leadership structures, or efficient internal systems.

Limited Revenue Diversification

Entities lack the tools or expertise to develop earned-income streams or alternative fundraising models.

Capacity Gaps

Widespread shortage of internal expertise in revenue modeling, compliance, and enterprise-level planning.
